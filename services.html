// Phase One

Pre Construction

Project Baseline Development

Defining the Owner's Project Requirements (OPR) ensures the design team solves the functional problem without "gold-plating." The design process is policed to align scope with the target budget, establishing the "Source of Truth" for cost and time that governs every decision downstream.

Constructability Audit

A forensic review of the 30%, 60%, and 90% drawing sets to identify clashes, gaps, and design errors.

Resolution is forced on paper, before it becomes a Change Order in the field.

Strategic Procurement

Identification and facilitation of direct procurement for critical path equipment — switchgear, chillers, steel — to lock in production slots early.

Decoupling these items from the GC's critical path mitigates supply chain risk and saves money.

Contract Structure & Negotiation

Commercial agreements are structured to eliminate the conflict of interest inherent in standard construction contracts, aligning Owner and Contractor incentives. Technical scope exhibits close every "grey area" between trades before a contract is signed.

Value Engineering & Descoping

Systematic reduction of project cost without sacrificing performance. Unnecessary specification requirements are stripped out, aligning the design with "First Principles" functionality.

Scope reductions are validated against OPR criteria to confirm no loss of functional performance.

Engineering Management

Coordinating across all engineering disciplines — civil, structural, mechanical, electrical, and process — ensuring each design package is fully integrated and free of cross-discipline conflicts before construction begins.

Strict adherence to Owner requirements is enforced throughout the design process.

02
// Phase Two

Construction Management

Executive Program Management

Setting the tone on site and enforcing hyper accountability across the General Contractor and Design Team. Acting as central command — enforcing contract compliance and removing roadblocks before they impact the schedule.

Capital Draw Audits

Preventing "front-loading" and cash-flow manipulation. Every dollar released is physically validated against the Schedule of Values (SOV) to ensure it matches field reality.

No verification, no payment.

Change Order Forensics

A granular, technical scrubbing of every potential change order. Root cause analysis distinguishes legitimate scope gaps from contractor errors, aggressively rejecting inflated pricing and non-allowable costs.

Critical Path Enforcement

The schedule is a project's north star. Tracking logic ties and float degradation in real-time identifies slippage weeks before it appears on a report, with acceleration strategies deployed to hold completion dates.

Quality Assurance & Field Engineering

Boots-on-the-ground inspection of critical details: concrete reinforcement, steel connections, and MEP routing. Ensuring the "as-built" reality matches the "as-designed" intent.

Sustained field presence creates accountability at every stage, catching deficiencies before they become costly rework.

RFI Management

Unanswered RFIs are silent schedule killers. Driving the full RFI lifecycle — logging, routing, and enforcing response deadlines across the design team.

Every RFI is scrutinized for scope and cost implications, identifying 2nd and 3rd order consequences before they reach the Owner's desk.

03
// Phase Three

Asset Activation

Integrated Systems Commissioning

Vektor, with our Partners, offers Cx consulting services from advisory through full turn-key delivery. Driving functional performance testing of mechanical, electrical, and process systems against peak-load design criteria to ensure operational reliability.

Tool Install & SOP Convergence

Eliminating the endless scope gaps between the Base Build and the Process. Managing the complex interface between construction turnover and equipment installation, ensuring specialty equipment is never stuck waiting for Base Build.

Commercial Closeout & Handover

Project closeout is the most critical financial milestone. Final payment retention is leveraged to force immediate resolution of construction defects, ensuring the warranty period does not begin until all work is approved.

04
// Phase Four · Emergency Services

Distressed Project Recovery

⚠ Rapid Response A Rapid Response Team deploys to stabilize projects that are burning through cash or running out of schedule. Within 72 hours, your exposure is audited, root cause identified, and a surgical recovery plan implemented.
01

Financial Exposure

A full forensic audit of current spend, committed costs, and projected overruns establishes the true financial picture and bounds total exposure.
02

Root Cause Analysis

Tracing the failure back to its origin — whether scope creep, contract structure, poor scheduling, or contractor performance — so the same problems don't recur under a recovery plan.
03

Recovery Plan

A surgical, Owner-first recovery strategy is built with hard milestones, defined accountability, and clear contractual levers to arrest the overrun and restore project momentum.
04

Partner Replacement

Where contractors or subcontractors are the root cause, termination, procurement, and onboarding of replacement partners is managed to minimize transition losses and protect schedule.
05

Re-Baseline

The project schedule and budget are rebuilt from the ground up, establishing a credible new baseline that reflects reality, not optimism, and holds all parties accountable to it.
